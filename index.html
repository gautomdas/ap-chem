Don't Let Jetstar Fade Away

PPG's Solution to Fading Paint

The problem:

Jetstar's bright paint is fading away.

This is a result of the organic compounds involved being particularly vulnerable to UV radiation from the sun. Short wavelength light (violet or blue) have more energy than long wavelength light (red or orange. The bright orange reflects the orange light, absorbing the more harmful blue and violet light. This high energy excites the outer molecules of the paint and causes chemical bonds - fading the orange color.

Resulting reactions:

The possible reactions that result in paint fading.

Chromophores are an atom or group whose presence is responsible for the color of a compound. Sunlight attacks the chromophores and changes their structure. The pigment then appears to be fading. Chromate reductions result. Oxygen generates free radicals which metal ions join to further the chromate reduction.

Solutions:

PPG's answer to the problem.

Use UV Protection in the form of paint sealants. A coating is not a full solution as it will eventually fade away. In addition to using a paint sealant to prevent the paint from oxidizing, make sure to keep the plane out of sun whenever possible. Also, use a physical cover to protect the plane from UV rays or keep in the shade as often as possible. Lastly, it is also good to wash the plan often to prevent oxygen build up.
